SECTION 1: DATA GOVERNANCE AND PROTECTION
================================================================================

1.1 CUSTOMER DATA CLASSIFICATION

All customer data must be classified according to the following categories:
- Public: Information available to general public
- Internal: Information for internal use only
- Confidential: Customer account and transaction data
- Restricted: Personally identifiable information (PII) and financial records

1.2 ENCRYPTION REQUIREMENTS

1.2.1 All customer personally identifiable information (PII) must be encrypted 
      at rest using AES-256 encryption or equivalent approved algorithm.

1.2.2 Data in transit shall be encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.

1.2.3 Encryption keys must be rotated every 90 days and stored in a certified
      Hardware Security Module (HSM).

1.3 DATA ACCESS CONTROLS

1.3.1 Access to customer financial records requires dual authorization from 
      both the requesting user's manager and a data steward.

1.3.2 Production database access must be approved by the Information Security 
      team and logged for audit purposes.

1.3.3 Customer service representatives shall not access account numbers or 
      social security numbers without an active support ticket.

1.4 CONSENT AND PRIVACY

1.4.1 Customer data shall not be processed for any purpose without explicit 
      written or electronic consent from the customer.

1.4.2 Customers must be provided with clear privacy notices explaining data 
      collection, usage, and retention practices.

1.4.3 Customer opt-out requests must be processed within 72 hours.

SECTION 2: TRANSACTION MONITORING AND ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING
================================================================================

2.1 TRANSACTION THRESHOLDS

2.1.1 Transactions exceeding $10,000 must be automatically flagged for 
      Anti-Money Laundering (AML) review.

2.1.2 Cash transactions exceeding $5,000 require enhanced customer 
      identification and verification.

2.1.3 High-risk transactions exceeding $50,000 must be approved by a senior 
      compliance officer before processing.

2.2 SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORTING

2.2.1 Suspicious activity must be reported to the compliance team within 
      24 hours of detection.

2.2.2 Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) must be filed with FinCEN within 
      30 days of initial detection.

2.2.3 International wire transfers to high-risk jurisdictions require 
      additional Know Your Customer (KYC) verification.

2.3 SANCTIONS SCREENING

2.3.1 All transactions must be screened against OFAC sanctions lists in 
      real-time before processing.

2.3.2 Matches or potential matches must halt transaction processing pending 
      manual review.

SECTION 3: DATA RETENTION AND ARCHIVAL
================================================================================

3.1 MANDATORY RETENTION PERIODS

3.1.1 Transaction records must be retained for 7 years from the date of 
      transaction as per federal banking regulations.

3.1.2 Customer account opening documentation shall be retained for 10 years 
      after account closure.

3.1.3 Loan documentation must be retained for 7 years after loan payoff or 
      charge-off.

3.1.4 Audit logs and system access records must be maintained for 5 years.

3.1.5 Email communications related to customer transactions must be archived 
      for 7 years.

3.2 SECURE ARCHIVAL

3.2.1 Archived data must maintain the same encryption standards as active data.

3.2.2 Archived records must be retrievable within 24 hours for regulatory 
      examination requests.

3.3 DATA DESTRUCTION

3.3.1 Data exceeding retention periods must be securely destroyed using 
      DOD 5220.22-M standards or equivalent.

3.3.2 Data destruction activities must be logged and certified by IT security.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: INCIDENT RESPONSE AND BREACH NOTIFICATION
================================================================================

4.1 INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION

4.1.1 Security incidents are classified as Low, Medium, High, or Critical 
      based on data sensitivity and scope.

4.1.2 Any incident involving customer PII or financial data is automatically 
      classified as High or Critical.

4.2 BREACH NOTIFICATION

4.2.1 Data breaches involving customer information must be reported to 
      regulatory authorities within 72 hours of discovery.

4.2.2 Affected customers must be notified within 30 days of breach discovery.

4.2.3 Breach notifications must include the nature of the breach, data types 
      affected, and remediation steps.

4.3 FORENSICS AND REMEDIATION

4.3.1 Critical incidents require immediate forensic investigation by the 
      cybersecurity team.

4.3.2 Incident post-mortems must be completed within 14 days and shared with 
      senior management.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: SYSTEM ACCESS AND AUTHENTICATION
================================================================================

5.1 PASSWORD POLICIES

5.1.1 All user passwords must be at least 12 characters long and include 
      uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters.

5.1.2 Passwords for privileged accounts shall be rotated every 90 days.

5.1.3 Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all remote access 
      and privileged accounts.

5.2 SESSION MANAGEMENT

5.2.1 User sessions must timeout after 15 minutes of inactivity.

5.2.2 Concurrent logins from different locations must be flagged for 
      security review.

5.3 PRIVILEGED ACCESS

5.3.1 Production system access requires just-in-time provisioning and 
      automatic de-provisioning after 8 hours.

5.3.2 All privileged access sessions must be recorded and retained for 
      audit purposes for 1 year.

================================================================================
SECTION 6: VENDOR AND THIRD-PARTY RISK MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

6.1 VENDOR DUE DILIGENCE

6.1.1 All third-party vendors with access to customer data must undergo 
      security assessment and approval by the vendor risk management team.

6.1.2 High-risk vendors require annual security audits and SOC 2 Type II 
      certification.

6.2 DATA SHARING

6.2.1 All data transfers to third parties require legal review and formal 
      data processing agreements.

6.2.2 Customer data shared with vendors must be encrypted in transit and 
      at rest.

6.2.3 Vendor access to customer data must be logged and monitored in 
      real-time.

6.3 VENDOR MONITORING

6.3.1 Vendor performance and security posture must be reviewed quarterly.

6.3.2 Security incidents at vendor sites must be reported to our compliance 
      team within 24 hours.

================================================================================
SECTION 7: COMPLIANCE AUDITING AND MONITORING
================================================================================

7.1 AUDIT REQUIREMENTS

7.1.1 Annual compliance audits are mandatory for all systems processing 
      customer data.

7.1.2 Internal audits must be conducted by independent auditors not involved 
      in system operations.

7.1.3 Audit findings must be remediated within 90 days of discovery, with 
      critical findings addressed within 30 days.

7.2 CONTINUOUS MONITORING

7.2.1 Automated compliance monitoring must be implemented for all critical 
      controls.

7.2.2 Control failures must trigger immediate alerts to the compliance team.

7.2.3 Compliance metrics must be reported to the Board of Directors quarterly.

7.3 POLICY ENFORCEMENT

7.3.1 Policy violations must be investigated and documented.

7.3.2 Repeat violations require escalation to senior management.

7.3.3 Willful policy violations may result in disciplinary action up to and 
      including termination.

================================================================================
SECTION 8: CHANGE MANAGEMENT AND SYSTEM CONTROLS
================================================================================

8.1 CHANGE APPROVAL

8.1.1 All production system changes require Change Advisory Board (CAB) 
      approval.

8.1.2 Emergency changes must be retroactively reviewed within 48 hours.

8.1.3 Changes affecting customer-facing systems require additional business 
      stakeholder approval.

8.2 SEGREGATION OF DUTIES

8.2.1 Developers must not have production deployment privileges.

8.2.2 The same individual shall not approve and execute high-risk transactions.

8.2.3 Access provisioning and access review must be performed by different 
      teams.

8.3 CODE MANAGEMENT

8.3.1 All code changes must undergo peer review before merging.

8.3.2 Production code deployments require sign-off from QA and security teams.

================================================================================
SECTION 9: EMPLOYEE TRAINING AND AWARENESS
================================================================================

9.1 MANDATORY TRAINING

9.1.1 All employees must complete annual compliance training covering data 
      protection, AML, and security policies.

9.1.2 Employees with access to customer data require additional privacy and 
      security training.

9.1.3 Training completion must be tracked and reported to compliance.

9.2 SPECIALIZED TRAINING

9.2.1 Compliance team members must maintain current certifications (CAMS, CRCM).

9.2.2 Security team members must complete security certifications (CISSP, CISA).

================================================================================
SECTION 10: REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

10.1 REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

This policy ensures compliance with:
- Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)
- USA PATRIOT Act
- Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)
- Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX)
- Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS)
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) - for EU customers

10.2 REGULATORY REPORTING

10.2.1 All required regulatory reports must be filed accurately and on time.

10.2.2 Late filings require explanation and senior management notification.

10.2.3 Regulatory examination requests must be responded to within required 
       timeframes, typically 48 hours.
